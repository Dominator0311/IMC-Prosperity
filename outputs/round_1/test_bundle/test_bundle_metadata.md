## Purpose

Ad-hoc directional experiment: keep the F5 / H1 wall-based ASH leg
(normal market-making behavior) and swap PEPPER to a **buy-and-hold**
strategy that takes any available ask up to the position limit and
then holds for the rest of the session. Serves as an upper-bound
reference for how much of PEPPER's official PnL is pure drift
mark-to-market vs market-making edge.

### Behavior summary

1. Tick 0: the strategy emits a taker buy intent with `buy_below = 1e9`
   (so any ask price crosses) and `sell_above = None`. The execution
   engine sweeps the ask book up to `max_aggressive_size = 50`. The
   risk manager clips to `position_limit - position = 50`.
2. Ticks 1..N: once position = 50, the risk manager clips the buy
   to 0. Since `sell_above = None`, no sell orders are emitted. Net
   result: the bot holds at +50 for the remainder of the session.
3. Final PnL = 50 × (end_mid − avg_entry_price) + ASH market-making
   PnL.

## Expected behavior (not a prediction of official PnL)

Upper-bound thought experiment on the 3-day local replay day-0 data:

- PEPPER mid at t=0:      ~11 998.5
- PEPPER mid at t=999 900: ~13 000
- Drift capture = 50 × (13 000 − entry_price)
- If entry_price ≈ 12 010 (first ask cross at t=0), drift capture
  ≈ 50 × 990 = **+49 500 PnL from drift alone**
- Plus ASH wall_mid PnL ≈ local 3-day ~+7 700 → test_bundle local
  combined ≈ +57 000 (1-day projection)

On the OFFICIAL Round-1 day where Alt PEPPER closed at +2 057 total
with flatten=0.9 riding ~+40 long, buy-and-hold at +50 would
recover a larger drift share at the cost of **entering at full-size
immediately** (one aggressive taker fill, no ladder-in).

**Do not treat the above as a promise.** Official fill model is not
the local replay. Buy-and-hold is a directional experiment; its
official PnL depends entirely on whether the observed +0.001/tick
drift holds. On a flat or reversing day it collapses. Upload only
to learn; do not promote.
