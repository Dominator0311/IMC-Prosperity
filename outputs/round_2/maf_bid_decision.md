# Round-2 MAF bid decision (batch D3)

Hierarchical opponent model + EV-maximising bid for the Round-2
Market Access Fee blind auction.

## Setup

- N teams in cohort: **200** (defensive lower bound; actual cohort is larger).
- MC trials per cell: **10,000**.
- Three opponent priors:
  - **naive-heavy** — 50% bid 0; 30% in [10, 100]; 20% in [200, 2k].
  - **balanced**    — 30% bid 0; 40% in [50, 500]; 25% in [500, 3k]; 5% in [3k, 10k].
  - **aggressive**  — 15% bid 0; 35% in [200, 1.5k]; 35% in [1.5k, 5k]; 15% in [5k, 20k].
- Default mixture weights (subjective): naive-heavy=45%, balanced=40%, aggressive=15%.
- Auction: top-half winners pay their bid; losers pay 0. Ties
  treated as losses (conservative).

## Private value v

Marginal value of full market access (extra +25% order-book volume):

- R1 final scored = +89 970 / 1M ticks. Expected R2 official
  similar magnitude (~+90k).
- PEPPER is pinned at +80 for 99.9% of the run → extra asks past
  the position limit are unconsumed → marginal access value on
  PEPPER is ~+8-12% of PEPPER PnL ≈ +6-10k.
- ASH has no binding limit → marginal access value ~+25% of ASH
  PnL ≈ +2-3k.
- **Central estimate v ≈ 10 000 XIRECs.**
- Sensitivity: also evaluate at v = 6 000 (PEPPER limit binds
  earlier than expected) and v = 15 000 (extra access on PEPPER
  helps more than estimated).

## Final recommendation (revised after official-test data)

**Bid `350` XIRECs.**

### Why the v estimate dropped from 10k → 3k

Original v=10 000 assumed PEPPER's +80 position limit would
rarely bind, so extra +25% access on PEPPER lifts it +8-12%.
Official-test data refutes that: PEPPER pins at +80 ~99% of
the run (open_no_short=True, base_long=80, ceiling=80, taker
execution → fills the long fast and holds). Extra ask access
past the limit is unconsumed. The ONLY PEPPER moments where
extra book volume helps are the first ~500 ticks of opening
accumulation — a tiny sliver of PnL.

Empirical decomposition (16 official test runs):
- ASH PnL ≈ +720/test → +7.2k scored. 25% uplift = +1.8k.
- PEPPER PnL ≈ +7100/test → +71k scored. 1-2% opening-phase
  uplift = +0.7-1.4k.
- **v_central ≈ 3 000 XIRECs** (range 2-4k).

### Decision at v = 3 000

- **EV-maximising bid (mixture-weighted):** `350` → EV +2220.
- **Robust (max floor-EV):** `1800` → floor EV +957,
  mixture EV +1164. Floor-protection costs
  ~1057 XIRECs (48% of mixture EV).
- **Bid 0:** EV = 0 (guaranteed loss — ties go to losers).
- **Old recommendation (bid 2300):** at v=3 000 this has EV
  ≈ (3000 - 2300) × 0.987 = +700, well below the EV-max bid.
  The old number was based on an over-stated v.

### Why not bid 0?

Bid 0 collapses to bid 0 vs every prior — all three priors
have at least 15% of opponents bidding 0, so you tie at the
bottom and lose (ties → losses per the wiki). EV is exactly
zero. Even at v=2 000, a small bid (200-400) captures the
naive-heavy quintile and yields +800-1500 XIRECs of EV.

Recommendation: **bid `350`** as the EV-max under v≈3k. If
you want floor-EV protection and trust the data less, escalate
to `1800` (gives up ~48% of mixture EV for
consensus-collapse resilience).

Confidence: **medium-high** on v (anchored to actual test
data); **medium** on opponent priors (subjective).
